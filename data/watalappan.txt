Sri Lankan Watalappan

Description: A rich, steamed coconut custard pudding sweetened with jaggery (kithul treacle) and spiced with cardamom and nutmeg. A signature dessert of Sri Lankan Malay heritage, common at festive occasions.

Ingredients (serves 6):
- 4 eggs
- 300 ml coconut milk
- 200g jaggery (kithul or palm jaggery), grated
- 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
- 1/4 tsp nutmeg powder
- 1/4 cup cashew nuts, chopped (optional)

Steps:
1. Melt jaggery with a splash of water over low heat until dissolved. Let cool slightly.
2. Whisk eggs, then combine with coconut milk, melted jaggery, cardamom, and nutmeg.
3. Strain the mixture into a heatproof dish. Sprinkle chopped cashews on top.
4. Cover with foil and steam for 45-60 minutes until set (a knife inserted should come out clean).
5. Cool completely before slicing and serving.

Spice level adjustment notes:
- This is a sweet dessert with no chili; "spice" here refers to warmth from cardamom/nutmeg only, not heat level.

Approximate nutrition (per serving):
Calories: 260 kcal | Protein: 6g | Fat: 14g | Carbohydrates: 28g | Sodium: 60mg
