Sri Lankan Deviled Chicken

Description: A spicy, tangy stir-fried chicken dish with bell peppers, onions, and a chili-ketchup based sauce. A fusion dish popular at parties and restaurants, showing Sri Lanka's colonial-era culinary influences.

Ingredients (serves 4):
- 500g boneless chicken, cubed
- 1 onion, cut into chunks
- 1 bell pepper, cut into chunks
- 2-3 green chilies, sliced
- 2 tbsp tomato ketchup
- 1 tbsp chili sauce
- 1 tsp soy sauce
- 1 tsp chili powder
- 2 garlic cloves, chopped
- 1 inch ginger, chopped
- 2 tbsp oil
- Salt to taste

Steps:
1. Marinate chicken with a little salt and chili powder for 15 minutes, then shallow fry until cooked and lightly browned. Set aside.
2. In the same pan, sauté garlic and ginger, then add onion, bell pepper, and green chilies. Stir-fry until slightly softened but still crisp.
3. Add ketchup, chili sauce, and soy sauce, stir to combine into a glossy sauce.
4. Toss in the fried chicken, mix well to coat evenly, cook for 3-4 minutes.

Spice level adjustment notes:
- Mild (1-2): use 1 green chili, reduce chili powder to a pinch.
- Medium (3): 2 chilies as listed.
- Hot (4-5): 3-4 chilies plus 1.5-2 tsp chili powder.

Approximate nutrition (per serving, medium spice level):
Calories: 320 kcal | Protein: 28g | Fat: 16g | Carbohydrates: 16g | Sodium: 560mg
