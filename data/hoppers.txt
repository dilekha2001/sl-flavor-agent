Sri Lankan Hoppers (Appa)

Description: Bowl-shaped fermented rice-flour and coconut milk pancakes, crispy at the edges and soft in the center. Often eaten for breakfast or dinner, sometimes with an egg cracked into the center (egg hoppers).

Ingredients (makes about 10):
- 2 cups rice flour
- 1 cup coconut milk
- 1/2 tsp yeast
- 1 tsp sugar
- 1 cup warm water
- Salt to taste
- Eggs (optional, one per hopper)

Steps:
1. Dissolve yeast and sugar in warm water, let sit for 10 minutes until foamy.
2. Mix rice flour, coconut milk, yeast mixture, and salt into a smooth batter. Let ferment for 3-4 hours or overnight.
3. Heat a small hopper pan (appachatti), swirl a ladle of batter around the sides to form a thin edge and pool in the center.
4. Cover with a lid and cook for 2-3 minutes until edges turn golden and crisp.
5. For egg hoppers, crack an egg into the center before covering.

Spice level adjustment notes:
- Hoppers themselves are not spiced; spice level is controlled by the accompanying sambol (see pol_sambol.txt) served alongside.

Approximate nutrition (per hopper, plain, without egg):
Calories: 130 kcal | Protein: 2g | Fat: 4g | Carbohydrates: 22g | Sodium: 90mg
