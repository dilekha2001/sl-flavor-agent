Sri Lankan Fish Curry (Malu Curry)

Description: A tangy, spicy fish curry made with tamarind and goraka (a sour fruit), common along the coastal regions of Sri Lanka.

Ingredients (serves 4):
- 500g fish steaks (tuna, seer fish, or mackerel)
- 1 tbsp roasted curry powder
- 1/2 tsp turmeric
- 1 tsp chili powder
- 2-3 pieces dried goraka (or 1 tbsp tamarind paste as substitute)
- 1 onion, sliced
- 3 garlic cloves
- 1 inch ginger
- 1 sprig curry leaves
- 200 ml thin coconut milk
- 100 ml thick coconut milk
- Salt to taste

Steps:
1. Soak goraka in warm water for 10 minutes.
2. In a clay pot or pan, layer fish with onion, garlic, ginger, curry leaves, turmeric, chili powder, and curry powder.
3. Add soaked goraka with its water, and thin coconut milk. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 10 minutes.
4. Add thick coconut milk, simmer gently for another 5 minutes without stirring vigorously (to keep fish pieces intact).
5. Season with salt, remove from heat.

Spice level adjustment notes:
- Mild (1-2): reduce chili powder to 1/4 tsp.
- Medium (3): 1 tsp chili powder as listed.
- Hot (4-5): add 2 slit green chilies plus 1.5-2 tsp chili powder.

Approximate nutrition (per serving, medium spice level):
Calories: 310 kcal | Protein: 26g | Fat: 19g | Carbohydrates: 6g | Sodium: 410mg
