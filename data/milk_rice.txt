Sri Lankan Milk Rice (Kiribath)

Description: Rice cooked in thick coconut milk until creamy, then set into a firm block and cut into diamond shapes. Traditionally eaten on special occasions and New Year's Day, often with seeni sambol or a katta sambol.

Ingredients (serves 4):
- 1 cup rice (short grain, if available)
- 1.5 cups water
- 1 cup thick coconut milk
- 1/2 tsp salt

Steps:
1. Cook rice with water until mostly absorbed and rice is soft.
2. Reduce heat, stir in coconut milk and salt.
3. Continue cooking on low heat, stirring frequently, until the mixture thickens into a creamy, sticky consistency.
4. Transfer to a flat dish, spread evenly, and let cool and set for at least 30 minutes.
5. Cut into diamond shapes before serving.

Spice level adjustment notes:
- Milk rice itself is unspiced; heat comes from accompaniments such as seeni sambol (see seeni_sambol.txt) or a chili-based lunu miris paste served on the side.

Calories: 300 kcal | Protein: 5g | Fat: 12g | Carbohydrates: 44g | Sodium: 210mg
