Sri Lankan Chicken Curry (Kukul Mas Curry)

Description: A rich, coconut-milk based chicken curry roasted with a dark blend of spices. A staple Sunday lunch dish across Sri Lanka, usually eaten with rice, string hoppers, or bread.

Ingredients (serves 4):
- 1 kg chicken, cut into curry pieces
- 2 tbsp roasted curry powder
- 1 tsp chili powder (adjust for spice level)
- 1 tsp turmeric powder
- 1 onion, sliced
- 4 garlic cloves, chopped
- 1 inch ginger, chopped
- 2 sprigs curry leaves
- 1 stick cinnamon
- 3 cardamom pods
- 400 ml coconut milk
- 2 tbsp coconut oil
- Salt to taste

Steps:
1. Marinate chicken with turmeric, chili powder, and salt for 20 minutes.
2. Heat coconut oil, add cinnamon, cardamom, curry leaves, onion, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until golden.
3. Add roasted curry powder and cook briefly until fragrant.
4. Add marinated chicken, stir to coat with spices, and cook for 5 minutes.
5. Pour in coconut milk, bring to a simmer, and cook uncovered for 25-30 minutes until chicken is tender and the gravy thickens.
6. Adjust salt and spice before serving.

Spice level adjustment notes:
- Mild (1-2): reduce chili powder to 1/4 tsp, omit fresh green chilies.
- Medium (3): use 1 tsp chili powder as listed.
- Hot (4-5): add 2-3 slit green chilies and increase chili powder to 2 tsp.

Approximate nutrition (per serving, medium spice level):
Calories: 420 kcal | Protein: 32g | Fat: 28g | Carbohydrates: 8g | Sodium: 480mg
